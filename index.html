About Us

Domestic Waste Management stands as a critical issue in contempory society, posing significant challenges to environmental sustainability and public health.

Improper handling of domestic waste leads to a myriad of environmental hazards such as pollution of air, water, and soil, degradation of ecosystems, and the proliferation of greenhouse gases, emissions, exacerbating climate change impacts.

Some basic stratergies that we relie on are as follows:

REFUSE

Refuse unnecessary items to reduce waste at the source: This means saying no to things you don't need or that will quickly become waste. For example, refusing single-use plastic bags or straws, or declining promotional items that will likely end up unused and discarded.

REDUCE

Reduce consumption by choosing products with less packaging or opting for reusable alternatives: This involves being mindful of the products you buy and selecting those that generate less waste. For instance, buying in bulk to reduce packaging waste, or choosing products made from recycled materials.

REUSE

Reuse items whenever possible to extend their lifespan and reduce the need for new products: Reusing items instead of throwing them away helps reduce waste. This can be as simple as using a refillable water bottle instead of buying bottled water, or repurposing jars and containers for storage.
